What is UX?

User experience design is a set of activities and philosophies that ensure users’ needs are understood, addressed, and – ideally – surpassed.

UX can encompass many other disciplines, and many other disciplines can encompass UX.

User experience design without user research is not user experience design.

User interface design is not user experience design.

...so why do the research and everything else that goes with it?

You are not your user!

Today's challenge!

Research

Why research?

In short: it's the foundation of understanding.

Some types of research

  • Stakeholder interviews
  • User interviews
  • Competitive analysis
  • Current state evaluation
  • Ethnographic research
  • Focus groups
  • Analytic analysis

It's research time.

Here's what we're going to do:

  1. Identify questions that help gain understanding.
  2. Break up into groups of 3.
  3. Designate an interviewee, a moderator, and a notetaker.

Questions

  1. Tell me about the last time you bought something online.
  2. What's the most important thing to you when buying jeans?
  3. If you could change one thing about buying jeans, what would it be?

Let's analyze the information we've got.

Some things we're looking for:

  • Trends
  • Common ideas, thoughts
  • Outliers

Note that we're not looking for solutions yet.

Information Architecture

What is IA?

Only the most important thing ever!

OK, that might be a bit much, but it's pretty darned vital.

Information architecture is a way of organizing, naming, and structuring information.

“Information architecture is the way that we arrange the parts of something to make it understandable.” - Abby Covert

  • Language
  • Iconography
  • Priority
  • Consistency

A good information architecture makes sense.
And we notice when it doesn't.

It's time to gather and organize.

Here's what we're going to do:

  1. Grab Post-Its and answer a few simple questions.
  2. Group and name items together.
  3. Prioritize what matters most.

One answer per Post-It, please.

  • What are the qualifiers for a pair of jeans?
  • What information matters?

Now let's group items and name them.

Break!

Sketching & Wireframing

Wireframing is a way to express a flow through a process or individual screens in a product.

Wireframes may be presented in different levels of fidelity, depending upon what’s needed.

  • Low fidelity: Sketches, napkins, Post-Its, whiteboards. Rough!
  • Medium fidelity: Additional detail where needed, especially for critical interactions/content/flows
  • High fidelity: Everything is accounted for!

It's time to sketch wireframes.

Here's what we're going to do: a design charrette!

  1. 6-up / 3-up / 1-up individually.
  2. 1-up as teams.
  3. Present your work.

What we'll be sketching

The product details page for our jean-selling site. This is the page where people can actually buy the jeans, see what they look like, and so forth.

Design Review

What's a design review?

It's a way to evaluate how successful or unsuccessful the design is as a part of your overall feedback cycle.

Design reviews can be formal, informal, or somewhere in-between. This isn’t quite a critique – where we’re all internally discussing and judging the work – but it’s close.
